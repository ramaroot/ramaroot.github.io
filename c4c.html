Mobile Case Study

Project Description

Crawl 4 The Cure is an event identified as it’s own non-profit organization which donates all it’s proceeds to the National Multiple Sclerosis Society - Upper Midwest Chapter.

Tools

Miro, InVision, Adobe XD

The Problem

The original design provided users with information that was difficult to access and a website experience that was not responsive between devices.

The Solution

We reorganized the information in a hierarchical order, being sure to clearly address: what, when, where, how, why, and who.

Top Research Design Iteration Prototype Final Thoughts

The Research Phase

Our group conducted 1-on-1 interviews as well as a Google survey of users on social media including Facebook and Instagram. Our research plan focused on these main topics:

I want to understand what information is most important to our users
I want to know what engages users when visiting a website
I want to understand what influences users toward supporting a causes

These topics directed our focus about keeping people informed about the event and increase registration. We also completed a competitor analysis. The results pointed strongly toward a desire for clear communication about the facts.

From there, we created our proto-persona. She took us on a journey to our user flow and initial wireframes.

The Design Phase

Design is never finished. But our wireframes were pretty good.

The Iteration Phase

We tested three users and received feedback to make iterations to our final prototype.

  • Register button at the top of the homepage
  • Register button in event details
  • An obvious home page button
  • Buttons under sponsor levels
  • Scroll indicator on front page
  • Back button on the contact form
  • Footer

Final Thoughts

Ultimately we achieved our goals with restructuring the site. Information was easier to find and the navigation and overall site was far more intuitive. There were sections we wanted to focus on more but had to cut due to constraints.
We didn’t get a chance to build out an “About” section. This section would include a more detailed section on the organization and why we raise money for Multiple Sclerosis. There would be a desktop version of the site as well. Similar, but wider. Many of the animations had hiccups and z-axis issues due to the complexity of the project. There was a strong push for animated jeeps that was sadly never realized.
